Come Discover The Beautiful City of Shenyang, China

The History of Shenyang

Shenyang has a dense history that dates back more than 2,300 years. It has been a significant city in China's history, once being the capital of the Qing Dynasty before later being moved to Beijing. Its past can be partially explored through breathtaking historical sites like the Shenyang Imperial Palace, the old city walls, and the tombs of Qing Dynasty emperors.

Shenyang's Many Attractions

A notable attraction in Shengyang is the Shenyang Imperial Palace, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site made to resemble Beijing's Forbidden City. Another attraction is the September 18th History Museum, which commemorates the Mukden Incident that led to one of the most destructive WWII conflicts, the Sino-Japanese War. The city's lush parks and the nearby Qipan Mountain are natural attractions within and around Shenyang for tourists and locals to both enjoy.

The Culture in Shenyang

The city's culture consists of a blend of traditional Manchu culture, an ethic group within China, and modern Chinese influences. The Shenyang dialect is a variant of Mandarin and it is widely spoken among the population of the city. Shenyang is known for its vibrant festivals like the Shenyang International Ice and Snow Festival, which shows off the city's cultural richness and character. Shenyang has a population of 7,681,000 as of 2023, making it the 54th largest city in the world at this time.
